# LinkedIn Platform Rules

## Character Limits

- Post body: 3,000 characters
- "See more" fold: ~210 characters (first 2-3 lines visible before fold)
- Everything before the fold is critical - this is your hook

## Post Structure

Follow the 3-2-1 rule:
- **3** hooks drafted, pick the strongest to lead
- **2** concrete insights or actionable takeaways
- **1** clear call-to-action

```
[Hook - first 2-3 lines must compel "see more" click]

[Body - 2-4 short paragraphs, each 2-3 sentences max]
[Use line breaks generously - no walls of text]
[Bold or structural formatting for scannability]

[Key takeaway or insight]

[CTA - question, link, or next step]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

## Hooks That Work

- **Contrarian hook:** challenges commonly held beliefs ("Most developers are wrong about X.")
- **Narrative hook:** starts in the middle of a story ("I almost deleted the entire codebase yesterday. Then I saw the test suite.")
- **Data hook:** surprising statistic or result
- **Confession hook:** vulnerable admission that builds trust

The first two lines determine whether anyone reads the rest. Spend disproportionate effort here.

## Tone

- Professional but human - not corporate speak
- Thought-leadership voice works well
- Personal stories and lessons learned drive engagement
- "Here's what I learned" > "We're pleased to announce"
- Founder-voice and team stories perform well for tech

## Hashtags

Include 1-3 relevant hashtags at the end of the post. Rules:
- Quality over quantity - 1-3 focused hashtags only
- Align with topic and audience, not vanity metrics
- Use as metadata to help the algorithm categorize, not as discovery mechanism
- Mix: 1 broad industry tag + 1-2 specific topic tags

## Emojis

More restrained than Twitter/X. Use for:
- Structural formatting (✅ bullet points, → arrows, 📌 pins)
- 1-3 total per post
- Never purely decorative
- Match professional tone - checkmarks and arrows > faces and fire

## Engagement Signals (2026 Algorithm)

- **Saves** are the most important engagement signal - content with lasting reference value gets extended distribution
- **Dwell time** is the #1 ranking factor - longer, valuable posts that keep people reading are rewarded
- **Golden hour:** first 90 minutes determine 70% of reach
- **Penalized:** posts with "Comment YES if you agree" or explicit engagement bait are algorithmically throttled
- Carousels get 24% engagement rate (4x text posts), consider suggesting carousel format for rich content

## Content Formats

- **Text posts:** 6.67% engagement rate - good for thought leadership and quick updates
- **Carousels:** 24.42% engagement rate - best for step-by-step content, comparisons, lists
- **Video (60-90s, captioned, vertical):** highest reach format in 2026
- When content suits it, suggest carousel or video format alongside the text post

## CTAs

- Soft, conversational CTAs work best
- Questions that invite genuine discussion > "like and share"
- "What's been your experience with X?" drives comments
- "Link in comments" for external links (avoids algorithm penalty for links in post body) - when using this approach, note in the copy that the user should post the link as a reply/comment manually

## Posting Times

- Best: Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM-4 PM local time
- Tech professionals: 7-9 AM Tuesday-Thursday for early-morning engagement
- Developer/startup communities show higher weekend activity than other sectors
- Golden hour (first 90 min) is critical - post when your audience is online
